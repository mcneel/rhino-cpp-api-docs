30  /// <summary>
31  /// PANOSE 1.0 font family kind
32  ///
33  /// The overall genre of the alphabet or script that is being described
34  /// is signified by the Family Kind digit. This digit consists of two parts:
35  /// the script kind identifier and the genre kind identifier.
36  /// In this case, the script identifier is Latin, and the genre type is described as Text,
37  /// Hand Written, Decorative or Symbol.
38 
39  /// The Family Kind digit is not controlled by specific measurements,
40  /// and there has been no attempt to mathematically determine the
41  /// appropriate category for a given font design. Visual and aesthetic
42  /// classification of Latin faces that are obviously script, decorative,
43  /// or symbol fonts is required.
44  /// </summary>
45  enum class FamilyKind : ON__UINT8
46  {
47  /// <summary>"Any" means match that digit with any available digit, which allows the mapper to handle distortable typefaces. </summary>
48  Any = 0,
49 
50  /// <summary>"No Fit" means that the item being classified does not fit within the PANOSE 1.0 classification system. </summary>
51  NoFit = 1,
52 
53  /// <summary>
54  /// To decide whether a font belongs to the Latin Text group follow the two step process below.
55  ///
56  /// A. Answer the following three questions. If they are all yes, then it belongs in this group.
57  /// If the answer is still ambiguous, go to step B.
58  ///
59  /// Does the font belong to a family that includes italic versions?
60  /// Most fonts in this group have a variety of weights and most include italic versions.
61  ///
62  /// Are the characters in the font made up of standard topologies constructed of standard parts?
63  ///
64  /// Is some portion of the font suitable for composing a paragraph of text?
65  ///
66  /// B. As a final tie breaker, look at the second digit of the Decorative (Section 4)
67  // and Handwritten (Section 3) families and see if there is something in them that
68  /// fits the font in question better.
69  /// https://monotype.github.io/panose/pan2.htm
70  /// </summary>
71  LatinText = 2,
72 
73  /// <summary>
74  /// Many fonts are clearly scripts and unrelated to any book face. On occasion,
75  /// though, the distinction gets rather vague. A good rule of thumb is that if
76  /// the cursive font is part a family that includes a book face, then it should
77  /// be classified in the Latin Text group. If it is freestanding with no obvious
78  /// related book face, then it falls into the Latin Hand Written group. This can
79  /// be a bit difficult to determine, since a font house may only choose to provide
80  /// the cursive from a larger family, so the classifier needs to think about the
81  /// face being processed and not do it purely by rote.
82  /// https://monotype.github.io/panose/pan3.htm
83  /// </summary>
84  LatinScript = 3,
85 
86  /// <summary>
87  /// Latin Decorative faces are those that are designed more for impact than readability.
88  /// Usually Decoratives are used singly or in small groups, for special purposes.
89  /// Small cap fonts are also included in this group because they have become unusual
90  /// enough to be considered special purpose fonts.
91  /// https://monotype.github.io/panose/pan4.htm
92  /// </summary>
93  LatinDecorative = 4,
94 
95  /// <summary>
96  /// Latin Symbol is where all the nonalphabetic fonts reside. These are fonts that can be loaded
97  /// like normal text fonts, but do not contain readable characters. Dingbats and specialized
98  /// symbol fonts are two examples.
99  /// https://monotype.github.io/panose/pan5.htm
100  /// </summary>
101  LatinSymbol = 5
102  };
